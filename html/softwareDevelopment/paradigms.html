Programming Paradigms

Programming paradigms are a way to classify programming languages based on their features. Languages can be classified into multiple paradigms.

declarative & imperative paradigms

Most languages can be categorized in declarative- and imperative- paradigms.

In an analogy, the waiter in an restaurant would be the declarative paradigm, he is only concerned with taking and bringing the right order not how the meal was made. The chef in the kitchen would be the imperative paradigm, because they are concerned how the meal is made.

paradigm focus explanation languages
declarative "what happens" It's a form of programming that removes as much as possible from the low-level details of how things happen. It tries to concern itself only with what happens. Work usually together with "imperative" programming languages. HTML, CSS, SQL
imperative "how something happens" Explicitly tells the computer what to do and how to do it. Java, C#, C++, JavaScript, Python, Ruby
paradigm advantages & disadvantage
declarative
  • ease of use and a shallow learning curve
  • often limited to a relatively small set of instructions
  • limited control over the results
imperative
  • much more control over what the programs can do
  • more complex in what they can achieve
  • more difficult to implement (syntax is stricter)
